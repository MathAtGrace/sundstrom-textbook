One way to formally define the biconditional statement is to simply define its truth table to be the following:

\begin{table}[h]
 \begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{|c|c ||c|}
     \hline
      $P$ & $Q$ & $P \leftrightarrow Q$\\ \hline
       T  &  T  & T \\ \hline
       T  &  F  & F \\ \hline
       F  &  T  & F \\ \hline
       F  &  F  & T \\ \hline
     \end{tabular}
     \caption{Truth Table for $P \leftrightarrow $Q}
     \label{Ta:bicond}
  \end{center}
\end{table}
