Multiplying both sides of inequality~(\ref{eq:5h}) by 2 gives
\begin{align} \notag
  2 \cdot k! &> 2 \cdot 2^k ,\text{  or} \\ \label{eq:5i}
  2 \cdot k! &> 2^{k + 1} .  \\ \notag
\end{align}
Now,  $k \geq 4$.  Thus,  $k + 1 > 2$, and hence  $(k + 1) \cdot k! > 2 \cdot k!$.  This means that
\begin{equation} \label{eq:5j}
\left( {k + 1} \right)! > 2 \cdot k!.
\end{equation}
