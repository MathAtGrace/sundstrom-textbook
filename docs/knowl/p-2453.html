In Figure 5.1.3, the elements of \(A\) are represented by the points inside the left circle, and the elements of \(B\) are represented by the points inside the right circle. The four distinct regions in the diagram are numbered for reference purposes only. (The numbers do not represent elements in a set.) The following table describes the four regions in the diagram.

Region Elements of \(U\) Set
1 In \(A\) and not in \(B\) \(A - B\)
2 In \(A\) and in \(B\) \(A \cap B\)
3 In \(B\) and not in \(A\) \(B - A\)
4 Not in \(A\) and not in \(B\) \(A^c \cap B^c\)
We can use these regions to represent other sets. For example, the set \(A \cup B\) is represented by regions 1, 2, and 3 or the shaded region in Figure 5.1.4.
