Let \(A\) and \(B\) be two nonempty sets. There are two projection functions with domain \(A \times B\text{,}\) the Cartesian product of \(A\) and \(B\text{.}\) One projection function will map an ordered pair to its first coordinate, and the other projection function will map the ordered pair to its second coordinate. So we define

\(p_1\x A \times B \to A\) by \(p_1( {a, b} ) = a\) for every \(( {a, b} ) \in A \times B\text{;}\) and
\(p_2\x A \times B \to B\) by \(p_2( {a, b} ) = b\) for every \(( {a, b} ) \in A \times B\text{.}\)
Let \(A = \left\{ {1, 2} \right\}\) and let \(B = \left\{ {x, y, z} \right\}\text{.}\)
