The notational details of this induction argument get quite involved. Many mathematicians prefer to prove TheoremÂ 8.12 using a property of the natural numbers called the Well-Ordering Principle. The Well-Ordering Principle for the natural numbers states that any nonempty set of natural numbers must contain a least element. It can be proven that the Well-Ordering Principle is equivalent to the Principle of Mathematical Induction.
