Let \(a\) be a positive real number. In Item 1 of Theorem 3.4.10, we proved that for each real number \(x\text{,}\) \(\left| x \right| \lt a\) if and only if \(-a \lt x \lt a\text{.}\) It is important to realize that the sentence \(-a \lt x \lt a\) is actually the conjunction of two inequalities. That is, \(-a \lt x \lt a\) means that \(-a \lt x\) and \(x \lt a\text{.}\)
