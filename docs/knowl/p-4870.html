The main results that we want to use now are Theorem 3.5.7 and Corollary 3.5.8. This corollary tells us that for any \(a \in \mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) \(a\) is congruent to precisely one of the integers 0, 1, or 2. Consequently, the integer \(a\) must be congruent to 0, 1, or 2, and it cannot be congruent to two of these numbers. Thus

  1. For each \(a \in \mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) \(a \in C[ 0 ]\text{,}\) \(a \in C[ 1 ]\text{,}\) or \(a \in C[ 2 ]\text{;}\) and

  2. \(C[ 0 ] \cap C[ 1 ] = \emptyset\text{,}\) \(C[ 0 ] \cap C[ 2 ] = \emptyset\) , and \(C[ 1 ] \cap C[ 2 ] = \emptyset\text{.}\)
