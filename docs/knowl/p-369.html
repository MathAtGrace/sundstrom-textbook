We assume that \(x\) and \(y\) are odd integers and will prove that \(x+y\) is an even integer. Since \(x\) and \(y\) are odd, there exist integers \(m\) and \(n\) such that \(x = 2m + 1\) and \(y = 2n+1\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} x + y \amp = \left( 2m + 1 \right) + \left( 2n + 1 \right)\\ \amp = 2m + 2n + 2\\ \amp = 2 \left( m + n + 1 \right)\text{.} \end{align*}

Since the integers are closed under addition, \(\left( m + n + 1 \right)\) is an integer, and hence the last equation shows that \(x + y\) is even. Therefore, we have proven that if \(x\) and \(y\) are odd integers, then \(x+y\) is an even integer.
