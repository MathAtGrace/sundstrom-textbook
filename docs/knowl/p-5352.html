We are now ready to prove the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic. The first part of this theorem was proved in Theorem 4.19 in Section 4.2. This theorem states that each natural number greater than 1 is either a prime number or is a product of prime numbers. Before we state the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, we will discuss some notational conventions that will help us with the proof. We start with an example.
