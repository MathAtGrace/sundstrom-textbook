Use mathematical induction to prove that

\begin{align*} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \cos^{\,2n}x \, dx \amp = \frac{1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdots (2n - 1)}{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 6 \cdots (2n)} \frac{\pi}{2} \text{ and }\\ \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \cos^{\,2n+1}x \, dx \amp = \frac{2 \cdot 4 \cdot 6 \cdots (2n)}{1 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdots (2n+1)}\text{.} \end{align*}

These are known as the Wallis cosine formulas.
