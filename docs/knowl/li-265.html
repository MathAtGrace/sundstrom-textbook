Item 3.

In light of the previous comment, when we speak of the quotient and the remainder when we “divide an integer \(a\) by the positive integer \(b\text{,}\)” we will always mean the quotient \(\left( q \right)\) and the remainder \(\left( r \right)\) guaranteed by the Division Algorithm. So the remainder \(r\) is the least nonnegative integer such that there exists an integer (quotient) \(q\) with \(a = bq + r\text{.}\)
