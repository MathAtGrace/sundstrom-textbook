The three main set operations are union, intersection, and complementation. Theorems 5.30 and Theorem 5.29 deal with properties of unions and intersections. The next theorem states some basic properties of complements and the important relations dealing with complements of unions and complements of intersections. Two relationships in the next theorem are known as De Morgan's Laws for sets and are closely related to De Morgan's Laws for statements.
