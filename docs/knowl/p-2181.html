The proof of Theorem 4.3.5 is Exercise 7. The recursive definition of a geometric series and Theorem 4.3.5 give two different ways to look at geometric series. Theorem 4.3.5 represents a geometric series as the sum of the first \(n\) terms of the corresponding geometric sequence. Another way to determine the sum of a geometric series is given in Theorem 4.3.6, which gives a formula for the sum of a geometric series that does not use a summation.

Theorem 4.3.6.

Let \(a, r \in \mathbb{R}\) and \(r \ne 1\text{.}\) If the sequence \(S_1 ,S_2 , \ldots ,S_n , \ldots\) is defined by \(S_1 = a\) and for each \(n \in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\) \(S_{n + 1} = a + r \cdot S_n\text{,}\) then for each \(n \in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\) \(S_n = a\left( {\dfrac{{1 - r^n }}{{1 - r}}} \right)\text{.}\)
