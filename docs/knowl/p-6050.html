Example: Since \(x\) is an odd integer, there exists an integer \(n\) such that

\begin{equation} x = 2n + 1\text{.}\tag{A.1} \end{equation}

Later in the proof, there may be a line such as Then, using the result in equation (A.1), we obtain \(\ldots \text{.}\) Please note that we should only number those equations we will be referring to later in the proof. Also, note that the word “equation” is not capitalized when we are referring to an equation by number. Although it may be appropriate to use a capital “E,” the usual convention in mathematics is not to capitalize.
