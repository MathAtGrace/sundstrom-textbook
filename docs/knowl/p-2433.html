We can extend the idea of consecutive integers (See Exercise 10 in Section 3.5) to represent four consecutive integers as \(m\text{,}\) \(m + 1\text{,}\) \(m + 2\text{,}\) and \(m + 3\text{,}\) where \(m\) is an integer. There are other ways to represent four consecutive integers. For example, if \(k \in \Z\text{,}\) then \(k - 1\text{,}\) \(k\text{,}\) \(k + 1\text{,}\) and \(k + 2\) are four consecutive integers.
