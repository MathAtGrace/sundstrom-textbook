By Theorem 9.19, the set \(\mathbb{Q}^+\) of positive rational numbers is countably infinite. So by Theorem 9.21, \(\mathbb{Q}^+ \cup \left\{ 0 \right\}\) is countably infinite. Now prove that the set \(\mathbb{Q}^-\) of all negative rational numbers is countably infinite and then use Theorem 9.24 to prove that \(\mathbb{Q}\) is countably infinite.
