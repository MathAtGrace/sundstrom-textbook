Corollary 8.2.7.

  1. Let \(a, b \in \mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) and let \(p\) be a prime number. If \(p \mid \left( {ab} \right)\text{,}\) then \(p \mid a\) or \(p \mid b\text{.}\)

  2. Let \(p\) be a prime number, let \(n \in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\) and let \(a_1 ,a_2 , \ldots , a_n \in \mathbb{Z}\text{.}\) If \(p \mid \left( {a_1 a_2 \cdots a_n } \right)\text{,}\) then there exists a natural number \(k\) with \(1 \leq k \leq n\) such that \(p \mid a_k\text{.}\)
