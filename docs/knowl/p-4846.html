It may seem tempting to divide both sides of equation (8.4) by \(b\text{,}\) but if we do so, we run into problems with the fact that the integers are not closed under division. Instead, we look at the other part of the hypothesis, which is that \(a\) and \(b\) are relatively prime. This means that \(\gcd( {a, b} ) = 1\text{.}\) How can we use this? This means that \(a\) and \(b\) have no common factors except for 1. In light of equation (8.4), it seems reasonable that any factor of \(a\) must also be a factor of \(c\text{.}\) But how do we formalize this?
