Answer 4.1.

Let \(u \in \left( {A \cup B} \right) \times C\text{.}\) Then, there exists \(x \in A \cup B\) and there exists \(y \in C\) such that \(u = \left( {x, y} \right)\text{.}\) Since \(x \in A \cup B\text{,}\) we know that \(x \in A\) or \(x \in B\text{.}\) In the case where \(x \in A\text{,}\) we see that \(u \in A \times C\text{,}\) and in the case where \(x \in B\text{,}\) we see that \(u \in B \times C\text{.}\) Hence, \(u \in \left( A \times C \right) \cup \left( B \times C \right)\text{.}\) This proves that \(\left( {A \cup B} \right) \times C \subseteq \left( {A \times C} \right) \cup \left( {B \times C} \right)\text{.}\) We still need to prove that \((A \times C) \cup (B \times C) \subseteq A \times (B \cup C)\text{.}\)
