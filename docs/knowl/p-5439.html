Let \(n\) be a natural number. Use the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic to explain why if \(n\) is composite, then there exist distinct prime numbers \(p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_r\) and natural numbers \(\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \ldots, \alpha_r\) such that

\begin{equation*} n = p_1^{\alpha_1} p_2^{\alpha_2} \cdots p_r^{\alpha_r} \qquad (1)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Using \(r = 1\) and \(\alpha_1 = 1\) for a prime number, explain why we can write any natural number greater than one in the form given in equation (1).
